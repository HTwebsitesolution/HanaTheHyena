# Hana Activity Pack - PDF Specifications

This folder contains the printable activity PDFs for Hana the Hyena website.

## PDF Files Required

### 1. hana-dream-bubbles-colouring.pdf
- **Purpose**: Colouring + imagination
- **Layout**: A4 portrait (210 × 297 mm)
- **Content**:
  - Header: Small Hana head icon/logo (left) + "Hana the Hyena · habeshapress.com" (right)
  - Title: "Hana's Big Dreams"
  - Prompt: "Colour Hana and her dream bubbles. In the empty bubble, draw something you dream about visiting."
  - Large line-art of Hana lying in her den
  - 3-4 dream bubbles: 2 with city elements (tall building, street lights, car), 1-2 empty for drawing
  - Bottom left note: "Can you tell someone about one of your dreams?"
  - Footer: "© Habesha Press – For personal and classroom use only. Not for resale."

### 2. hana-dinner-table-colouring.pdf
- **Purpose**: Colouring + food discussion
- **Layout**: A4 portrait
- **Content**:
  - Header (same as above)
  - Title: "Dinner with New Friends"
  - Prompt: "Colour the dinner table with injera, doro wat, and gomen besiga. Add your favourite food to the table!"
  - Line-art of family + Hana around table
  - Some plates blank for kids to draw their own foods
  - Bottom right box: "My favourite food is: _______"
  - Footer (same as above)

### 3. hana-journey-maze.pdf
- **Purpose**: Maze based on Harar → Addis spread
- **Layout**: A4 portrait
- **Content**:
  - Header (same as above)
  - Title: "Help Hana Find the City"
  - Prompt: "Start in Harar and help Hana find her way through the maze to Addis Ababa. Don't get stuck in the forest!"
  - Large maze covering most of page
  - Start icon: Small Hana near "Harar" gate
  - Exit icon: Outline of Addis skyline with star
  - Optional: Tiny icons along correct path (tree, river, hill, streetlight)
  - Footer (same as above)

### 4. hana-spot-the-difference-journey.pdf
- **Purpose**: Observation + colouring
- **Layout**: A4 portrait
- **Content**:
  - Header (same as above)
  - Title: "Spot the Differences – On the Way to Addis"
  - Prompt: "Look carefully at the two pictures. Circle the 7 differences you can find. Then colour both scenes."
  - Two nearly identical line-art images (side-by-side or stacked)
  - Differences: missing tree, extra cloud, different car, moved star, etc. (7 total)
  - Bottom: "Which picture looks more like the way you imagine Hana's journey?"
  - Footer (same as above)

### 5. hana-feelings-timeline.pdf
- **Purpose**: SEL / emotional literacy
- **Layout**: A4 portrait
- **Content**:
  - Header (same as above)
  - Title: "Hana's Feelings Journey"
  - Prompt: "Look at the moments from the story. Draw Hana's face to show how she feels in each one."
  - Timeline with 5-6 boxes (horizontal or vertical):
    1. Dreaming in Harar (house icon)
    2. Arriving in Addis (city icon)
    3. Standing alone on the street (street icon)
    4. Being invited to dinner (table icon)
    5. Eating with the family (food icon)
    6. Walking home again (path icon)
  - Each box: icon + "Hana feels ________" + blank circle for expression
  - Footer (same as above)

### 6. hana-postcard-from-addis.pdf
- **Purpose**: Writing + creativity
- **Layout**: A4 portrait
- **Content**:
  - Header (same as above)
  - Title: "Postcard from Hana"
  - Prompt: "Imagine Hana is writing to her friends back in Harar. What does she tell them about the big city and the family who invited her to dinner?"
  - Left half: Blank "front" of postcard with faint Addis skyline at bottom
  - Right half: "Back" with lines for message (4-6 lines) + fake stamp box with hyena icon
  - Footer (same as above)

### 7. hana-activity-pack-all-in-one.pdf (Optional)
- **Purpose**: Combined pack
- **Layout**: A4 portrait
- **Content**:
  - First page: Cover with "Hana the Hyena · Activity Pack" + small Hana picture + note
  - Following pages: All 6 individual activities collated

## Design Specifications

### General Requirements
- **Page size**: A4 portrait (210 × 297 mm)
- **Margins**: 12-15 mm on all sides
- **Style**: Clean black outlines, white/very light background
- **Header**: Small, unobtrusive
  - Left: Small Hana head icon or logo
  - Right: "Hana the Hyena · habeshapress.com"
- **Footer**: Tiny text, centred
  - "© Habesha Press – For personal and classroom use only. Not for resale."

### Design Notes
- Keep backgrounds white or very light for printability
- Use thick outlines suitable for younger children
- Ensure all text is readable when printed in black and white
- Test print quality before finalizing

## Production Notes

These PDFs should be created using:
- Canva (with line-art from illustrator or AI-generated)
- Adobe Illustrator/InDesign
- Or similar design tools

The line-art should be based on actual scenes from "The Hyena Who Went To Dinner" book.
